HANDSHAKE

About

This Friday, Swedish band Handshake will release their first single, Waiting Still, from their upcoming debut album Runaway. The song has also been turned into a poignant video, directed by Jimmy Olsson and featuring actor Johan Hedenberg. The video had its premiere yesterday at the music website HYMN, who wrote: ”Experimental electronica combines beautifully with dark Americana vocals”.

Waiting Still is a melancholy reflection on the difficulty of coming to terms with life as it is slowly beginning to fade, and you realize your lofty ambi- tions remain unfulfilled.

The single will be out October 30. The album, Runaway, will be released by Red Olive Music on Spotify on November 30.

Handshake was sparked a few years ago in Malmö, when electronica duo Patrik Ebbersten and Carl-Johan Elger had the idea to add vocals to their instrumental work. By accident, they ran into singer Peder Gravlund (Shooting John) in a bar and asked him to give it a try. He immediately took to the arrangements and had little trouble coming up with melodies for the arrangements. They were all pleased with the result.

The project developed into a meeting of two worlds: Gravlund’s dark Ame- ricana-vocals combined with Elger/Ebbersten’s experimental electro-tunes. Either he added vocals to their finished arrangements, or they added sup- port to his acoustic songs.

Slowly something came into being – at times overcoming distances, when Gravlund moved to the fjords of Norway for a spell. Finally, they had enough songs and decided to involve Gustav Haggren (Case Conrad) as co-producer and studio musician to add a final touch. Haggren also finis- hed a mix of the final selection of eight songs, before they were sent to Emil Isaksson for mastering at Studio Möllan in Malmö.

Runaway was born.
